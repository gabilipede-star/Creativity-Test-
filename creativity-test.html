Creativity Test

An Exploration of Imaginative Thinking and Storytelling

About This Test

This test measures your creative thinking—your ability to imagine new causes and effects in narrative form. You will respond to four creative tasks, each designed to explore different dimensions of imaginative thought.

Aim to make your responses:

  • Original — venture beyond everyday scenarios
  • Internally consistent — unusual is encouraged, but logic should hold within your story world
  • Clearly articulated — express ideas in complete, well-structured sentences

There are no "correct" answers. We're interested in how your imagination works and how effectively you can communicate novel ideas.

01

World-Building

Reimagine the rules of reality

Choose one scenario and write 5–7 sentences exploring what happens next:

1. The moon begins to shine brightly during the day
2. Time starts running backward for one hour every night
3. People's dreams begin to appear on public screens where everyone can see them
CONSIDER:
  • What new problems or opportunities might emerge?
  • How would daily life be transformed?
  • What new social norms or customs might develop?
02

Perspective-Shifting

See the world through another's lens

Imagine waking up tomorrow as someone you know well—your best friend, your teacher, or another person in your life.

Question: How would this person approach a problem you're currently facing? Write 5–7 sentences explaining their thinking and actions.

EXAMPLE:
  • "If I were my best friend, I would study in the library instead of my room because she believes that changing her environment helps her focus and prevents distractions."
03

Action-Generating

Place a historical or fictional figure in modern Japan

Choose one character and write 5–7 sentences describing what they try to do and what unexpected result occurs:

Albert Einstein – Theoretical physicist who revolutionized our understanding of space and time
Frankenstein's Creature – The intelligent but isolated being from Mary Shelley's classic novel
Doraemon – The well-meaning robot cat from the future whose gadgets often backfire
Oda Nobunaga – The ambitious daimyō who sought to unify Japan during the Sengoku period
Miyamoto Musashi – Legendary swordsman and philosopher from feudal Japan
Astro Boy (Tetsuwan Atom) – The compassionate robot hero with extraordinary abilities
CONSIDER:
  • What would this character attempt if they appeared in contemporary Japan?
  • What aspects of modern life would surprise or confuse them?
  • What unintended or ironic consequence might result from their actions?
04

Reflection

Analyze your creative process

Review your three stories and answer these questions in 3–5 sentences each:

1. Which story feels most original or creative to you?

2. Why do you consider it original?

3. What new cause-and-effect relationship did you explore in that story?

Before You Submit

Remember: There are no wrong answers—only different ways of imagining.
